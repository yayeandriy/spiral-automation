Roboscope

Introduction

Wind turbine blade production follows a precise sequence of steps including shell lay-up, resin infusion, and curing—each governed by stringent acceptance standards. However, when inspectors rely on tape measures, calipers and paper check-sheets to record faults such as wrinkles, delamination and foreign inclusions, the process often breaks down: defects can be recorded inaccurately or incompletely, assessment criteria drift between different factories, handovers of complex repairs to engineering teams become confused and delayed, and there is no centralised repository for inspection data to support trend analysis or root-cause

investigations.

Roboscope is a digital system designed for technicians conducting visual inspections of shells and other components between infusion and closure. With a simple smartphone app, operators point at each defect and Roboscope’s on-device computer-vision model immediately captures its exact dimensions and spatial coordinates relative to the leading edge and blade root. As defects are recorded, the app cross-references the blade’s current lay-up stage and the configured XFMEA risk framework to surface tailored repair instructions, while all annotated data and high-resolution images are streamed in real time via a REST API to a centralized engineering portal. In that portal, teams review progress against interactive 3D blade models, filter by defect type or severity, investigate individual records, and export consolidated logs for ERP or MES integration.

By combining augmented-reality-based capture with advanced telemetry and centralized analytics, Roboscope streamlines visual inspections, cuts cycle times, and raises overall manufacturing efficiency throughout the blade-production lifecycle.

In this proof-of-concept you alternate between two roles — the Quality Inspector on the shop floor (mobile app) and the Engineer in the office (web portal). If you’re the only participant, simply switch between the two views on your device and browser.

oLaunch Roboscope, tap the + icon, choose Inspection, pick a part type, then tap Create →Start.

oYou will enter the screen with your camera on.

Tips & Troubleshooting

1.Lighting: Maintain ≥ 600 lux for reliable AR tracking.

2.Resume: Only the latest session can be resumed; older sessions must be deleted and

restarted.

3.Origin Placement: Always position at or beyond the blade root for correct spatial

references.

This update incorporates various improvements based on "requests from the population". The system now utilizes only two primary modes for defect placement and adjustment: Two points and Edge Mod.

General System Enhancements

Tracking while LockedA notable feature is that if you lock an axis (e.g., X or Z), the system still continues to track the underlying surface. It maintains the locked axis while simultaneously moving along the surface.

Two Points Mode (Point Adjustment)

While the general operation of the Two Points Mode has not significantly changed, the ability to adjust the points has been enhanced:

1. Adjustment, Not Replacement: The system now focuses on adjustment of existing points, rather than full replacement.

2. Moving Points: You can now move any of the points.

3. Locking Axes: Points can be locked on specific axes (e.g., Z).

4. Placement and Snapping:

◦ The point works best when directed at the H (likely referring to an edge or corner). Directing it at the H ensures it places the marker "perfectly straight" and centered.

◦ The system will "snipe" (snap) to the surface, even in free mode.

◦ Points can be dragged for placement.

5. Angle Maintenance: If the second point is not moved, the placement angle will generally remain unchanged.

6. Known Issue (Y-Axis): Movement along the Y-axis is currently non-functional. This is because attempting to move the point on the Y-axis lifts it above the surface. This issue is slated for correction.

Edge Mode (Edge Mod)

The Edge Mode is designed for placing markers specifically along edges:

  • Status: Edge Mod is currently described as "raw" ("сыроватый") and should be treated as experimental. It is advised not to consider it fully workable yet.
  • Functionality: In this mode, the system attempts to find the intended edge surface and place the marker.
  • Current Limitations: The system sometimes fails to locate the surface when attempting placement.

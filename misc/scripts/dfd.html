Game‑Changer for HMLV Manufacturing

High‑mix, low‑volume production creates a unique inspection challenge: too many product variations, and not enough scale to justify traditional machine vision. Fixed‑camera systems demand custom programming, fixtures and calibration for each SKU – overhead that quickly breaks the economics of short runs. Manual inspection, meanwhile, struggles with variability and knowledge transfer across shifts.

Key HMLV Inspection Challenges

  • Excessive Setup – Tooling and calibration overhead slows short runs.
  • Knowledge Silos – Critical expertise locked with a few senior inspectors.
  • Weak ROI – Automation economics break down at low volumes.
  • Inconsistent Quality – Results vary widely across shifts and teams.
  • FAI Delays – First article checks hold up production flow.
  • Paper Burden – Manual records and reporting drain inspection time.

Our Mobile‑First Solution

AVIS runs on smartphones and tablets, resilient to jitter, rotation, and operator movement. Inspections can happen directly at the machine, inside complex structures, or anywhere on the shop floor. Real‑World Use Cases

Assemblies – Component Presence & Surface Condition

A proof‑of‑concept validated positional accuracy and surface condition across a mix of screws, knobs and brackets. The assembly was divided into zones; each zone is handled by its own validation model. Transitions are seamless to the operator.

Markings – Text & Label Verification

The system first detects a pipe variant, then reads the factory‑applied label using OCR. It cross‑checks the part identity with the label, producing a clear pass or fail.

Accuracy Check – 10 mm Displacement Demo

A simplified shape configuration shows how a 10 mm displacement triggers a failure. Tighter tolerances are achievable with more precise part definitions.

Technology Under the Hood

Detection models are trained through a synthetic rendering pipeline, eliminating manual labeling. Operators demonstrate correct and incorrect configurations on the shop floor; the system learns real‑world variations and tolerance limits directly from those examples.
